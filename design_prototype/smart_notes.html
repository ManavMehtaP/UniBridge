Executive Summary

This lecture explores the fundamental principles of **Neural Plasticity**, specifically focusing on how synaptic connections strengthen or weaken over time in response to increases or decreases in their activity.

The core takeaway is that the brain is not a static organ but a dynamic system capable of structural reorganization based on experience, learning, and environmental stimuli.

Important Points

  • Synapses are the primary sites of information storage in the nervous system.

  • Experience-dependent plasticity is most vigorous during critical periods of development.

  • NMDA receptors play a crucial role in inducing synaptic changes.

  • The "Use it or Lose it" principle governs neural pathway maintenance.
